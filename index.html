Overview

This project looks at offensive and defensive success together, using Gold Gloves and Silver Sluggers relative to career length. Instead of total awards, the focus is on how often players earned them over time.

The filters help surface different views of the data — from meaningful trends to edge cases like one-season, 100% players.

The quadrant chart groups players by their combination of offensive and defensive success. Over a full career, no player lives in the top-right "gold" zone, though some pass through it at certain points or with specific teams.

The line chart shows how those rates change over time. Across the population, success tends to peak around the 10-year mark.

The leaderboard includes a Rating from 0–100, which weights Gold Gloves and Silver Sluggers equally. This creates a single measure of overall two-way success.

Award totals are often discussed, but rarely in the context of career length. This view highlights how sustained performance compares across players.
